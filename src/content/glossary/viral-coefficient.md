## What is the Viral Coefficient?

The viral coefficient is a metric used to measure how many new users are generated by each existing user through referrals or word-of-mouth. It indicates how quickly a product or service can grow organically through its users.

## An Example to Understand Viral Coefficient

If every user invites 2 new users to join a product, and each of those 2 new users also invites 2 more, the viral coefficient is 2, meaning the product has the potential for exponential growth.

## Benefits of Using Viral Coefficient

- **Measures Organic Growth**: The viral coefficient helps businesses understand the rate at which their product or service is growing through user referrals and word-of-mouth.
- **Identifies Growth Potential**: A high viral coefficient indicates strong product-market fit and potential for rapid, sustainable growth.
- **Optimizes Referral Programs**: It provides insights into the effectiveness of referral programs and how to improve them.

## Why is Viral Coefficient Important for Startups and SaaS?

For startups and SaaS businesses, a high viral coefficient is a powerful growth lever. It helps achieve rapid, low-cost user acquisition by leveraging the existing customer base to generate more users.

## FAQs

### How Do I Calculate the Viral Coefficient?

The viral coefficient is calculated by multiplying the average number of invites each user sends by the conversion rate of those invites (how many of the invites become new users).

### What Is a Good Viral Coefficient?

A viral coefficient greater than 1 indicates exponential growth, while a coefficient less than 1 means the product is not growing virally.
