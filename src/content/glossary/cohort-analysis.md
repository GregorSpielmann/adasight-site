## What is Cohort Analysis?

Cohort analysis is a method of analyzing the behavior and performance of a group of users who share a common characteristic or experience within a specific time period. This technique helps businesses understand how different user groups engage with their product over time.

## An Example to Understand Cohort Analysis

If a SaaS company launches a new feature, cohort analysis can be used to compare how users who adopted the feature in the first week perform over the next three months versus those who adopted it later. This helps determine the feature’s impact on retention and engagement.

## Benefits of Using Cohort Analysis

- **Identifies Trends**: Helps reveal patterns in user behavior and product adoption over time.
- **Improves Retention**: By analyzing cohorts, businesses can find ways to retain specific groups of users more effectively.
- **Informs Product Decisions**: Provides valuable insights on which features or experiences lead to long-term success.

## Why is Cohort Analysis Important for Startups and SaaS?

For startups and SaaS businesses, cohort analysis enables better segmentation and a deeper understanding of how user behaviors change over time. This data helps fine-tune marketing and product strategies to improve customer retention and lifetime value.

## FAQs

### How Are Cohorts Defined in Cohort Analysis?

Cohorts are typically defined by user characteristics, such as the time of sign-up, the first product interaction, or behavior patterns.

### What Can Cohort Analysis Tell Me About User Retention?

It shows how different groups of users engage with your product over time, helping identify which cohorts stay longer and which churn faster.
