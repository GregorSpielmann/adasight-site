## What is Statistical Power?

Statistical power refers to the probability that a statistical test will correctly reject the null hypothesis when it is false. It is important for ensuring that experiments and tests are sensitive enough to detect meaningful effects.

## An Example to Understand Statistical Power

In an A/B test, if the statistical power is 80%, there’s an 80% chance that the test will correctly identify a true difference between the two variations being tested.

## Benefits of Using Statistical Power

- **Ensures Reliable Results**: High statistical power reduces the likelihood of Type II errors (failing to detect a true effect).
- **Optimizes Experiment Design**: By understanding statistical power, businesses can design experiments that are more likely to yield valid conclusions.
- **Reduces Risk of False Negatives**: Helps avoid situations where a meaningful change is overlooked due to insufficient testing sensitivity.

## Why is Statistical Power Important for Startups and SaaS?

For startups and SaaS businesses, ensuring high statistical power in experiments helps avoid wasting resources on tests that are too weak to detect important changes. It leads to more confident, data-driven decision-making.

## FAQs

### How Do I Increase Statistical Power?

Increasing sample size, improving measurement precision, and reducing variability can increase statistical power.

### What Is an Acceptable Level of Statistical Power?

Typically, a statistical power of 80% or higher is considered acceptable in most research and business experiments.
