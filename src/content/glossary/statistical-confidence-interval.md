## What is Statistical Confidence Interval?

A statistical confidence interval is a range of values used to estimate the true population parameter, with a certain level of confidence. It is often used in hypothesis testing and statistical inference.

## An Example to Understand Statistical Confidence Interval

If a survey finds that 65% of users prefer a new feature, with a 95% confidence interval of ±5%, this means that, with 95% certainty, the true percentage of users who prefer the feature lies between 60% and 70%.

## Benefits of Using Statistical Confidence Interval

- **Provides a Range**: Unlike a single point estimate, a confidence interval offers a range of possible values, helping to account for uncertainty.
- **Increases Accuracy**: It helps provide more reliable estimates of population parameters.
- **Guides Decision-Making**: Businesses can make more informed decisions by considering the range of possible outcomes rather than just point estimates.

## Why is Statistical Confidence Interval Important for Startups and SaaS?

For startups and SaaS businesses, confidence intervals help to measure the uncertainty around key metrics, such as customer satisfaction or market share, providing a clearer picture of potential outcomes.

## FAQs

### How Is a Confidence Interval Calculated?

It is calculated using sample data, standard deviation, and the sample size, along with the desired confidence level (e.g., 95%).

### What Does a Wider Confidence Interval Indicate?

A wider interval suggests more uncertainty about the true value of the population parameter, often due to a small sample size or high variability.
