## What is Causal Inference?

Causal inference is the process of determining whether and how one variable or action directly causes another. In the context of product analytics, it helps businesses understand the cause-and-effect relationships between user behavior and business outcomes.

## An Example to Understand Causal Inference

If a SaaS company introduces a new feature and sees an increase in user engagement, causal inference helps determine whether the feature itself caused the increase, rather than other factors like seasonal trends or marketing efforts.

## Benefits of Using Causal Inference

- **Improves Decision-Making**: Helps businesses understand what causes changes in key metrics, allowing for more effective strategies.
- **Accurate Predictions**: By understanding causal relationships, businesses can make more accurate forecasts and optimize their efforts.
- **Drives Growth**: Identifying true causes of success allows companies to focus on actions that will have the most significant impact.

## Why is Causal Inference Important for Startups and SaaS?

For startups and SaaS companies, causal inference helps to pinpoint which product changes or marketing strategies directly impact user behavior, growth, and retention. This enables companies to allocate resources effectively and make data-driven decisions.

## FAQs

### How is Causal Inference Different from Correlation?

Causal inference focuses on understanding cause-and-effect relationships, while correlation only identifies patterns without implying causality.

### What Methods are Used for Causal Inference?

Techniques include randomized controlled trials (RCTs), observational studies, and statistical modeling.
