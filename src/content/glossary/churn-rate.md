## What is Churn Rate?

Churn rate is the percentage of customers who stop using a product or service within a given period. It is a critical metric for understanding customer retention and the long-term sustainability of a business.

## An Example to Understand Churn Rate

If a SaaS company has 1,000 customers at the start of the month and loses 50 customers by the end, the churn rate for the month is 5% (50/1,000).

## Benefits of Using Churn Rate

- **Measures Retention**: Helps businesses assess the effectiveness of retention strategies and the overall health of customer relationships.
- **Identifies Areas for Improvement**: A high churn rate indicates that there are issues with the product or service that need attention.
- **Predicts Growth**: A declining churn rate suggests improved customer loyalty, which is a good indicator of potential future growth.

## Why is Churn Rate Important for Startups and SaaS?

For startups and SaaS businesses, churn rate is one of the most critical metrics. It directly impacts revenue and customer lifetime value, making it essential to identify and address churn quickly to sustain long-term growth.

## FAQs

### What Is a Healthy Churn Rate for SaaS?

While churn rates vary by industry, a typical SaaS company aims for a churn rate under 5% per month.

### Can the Churn Rate Be Begative?

Yes, negative churn happens when a company acquires new customers at a rate that offsets the churn, leading to net customer growth.
