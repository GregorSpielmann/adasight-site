## What is Customer Lifetime Value (CLV or LTV)?

Customer Lifetime Value (CLV or LTV) is the predicted net revenue a customer will generate over the entire duration of their relationship with a business. It helps businesses estimate how much they can invest in customer acquisition and retention while remaining profitable.

## An Example to Understand Customer Lifetime Value (CLV or LTV)

If a customer subscribes to a SaaS product for 12 months at $100 per month, their LTV would be $1,200. This figure helps the business determine how much to spend on acquiring similar customers.

## Benefits of Using Customer Lifetime Value (CLV or LTV)

- **Guides Marketing and Sales Strategy**: Knowing LTV helps businesses allocate resources effectively and decide how much to invest in acquiring new customers.
- **Predicts Revenue**: CLV helps businesses predict future revenue from existing customers, which aids in long-term planning.
- **Improves Customer Retention**: By focusing on increasing LTV, businesses can prioritize efforts to retain customers and increase repeat purchases or renewals.

## Why is Customer Lifetime Value (CLV or LTV) Important for Startups and SaaS?

For startups and SaaS businesses, CLV is a critical metric for understanding the long-term value of each customer. It helps guide decisions about customer acquisition costs, retention strategies, and overall growth targets.

## FAQs

### How Do I Calculate CLV?

CLV can be calculated using the formula: LTV = (Average Purchase Value) × (Purchase Frequency) × (Customer Lifespan).

### What’s a Good LTV for SaaS?

A good LTV varies by company, but it should generally be at least 3 times your Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) for sustainable growth.
