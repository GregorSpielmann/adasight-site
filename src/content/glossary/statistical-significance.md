## What is Statistical Significance?

Statistical significance is a measure of whether the results of an experiment or test are likely due to the effect being studied rather than random chance. A result is statistically significant if it is unlikely to have occurred under the null hypothesis.

## An Example to Understand Statistical Significance

In an A/B test comparing two landing pages, a p-value of 0.03 means the result is statistically significant, with a 3% probability that the difference is due to random chance.

## Benefits of Using Statistical Significance

- **Validates Results**: Ensures that observed results are not due to random variation, providing confidence in the findings.
- **Guides Business Decisions**: Statistically significant results offer reliable data to make informed decisions about product changes or marketing strategies.
- **Reduces Uncertainty**: By determining statistical significance, businesses can confidently act on data, knowing the results are reliable.

## Why is Statistical Significance Important for Startups and SaaS?

For startups and SaaS companies, statistical significance is critical for ensuring that changes (e.g., product features or marketing campaigns) have a true, measurable impact, minimizing the risk of making decisions based on false or inconclusive data.

## FAQs

### What P-value is Considered Statistically Significant?

A p-value of less than 0.05 is typically considered statistically significant, meaning there is less than a 5% chance the result is due to random variation.

### How do I Interpret Statistical Significance?

A statistically significant result means that the effect observed is likely real and not due to chance, providing confidence in the conclusions drawn from the data.
