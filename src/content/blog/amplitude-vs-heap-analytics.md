Amplitude and Heap are [product analytics tools](https://www.adasight.com/partner/amplitude) that helps businesses understand user behavior and make data-driven decisions. Through personalized insights and interactive visualizations, these tools pinpoint areas where users struggle or abandon tasks and highlight preferred functionalities. These tools help marketing executives determine the user-preferred features and the effectiveness of their marketing efforts, allocate resources accordingly, and improve conversion rates.

When comparing [Amplitude](https://www.adasight.com/blog/understanding-the-dau-mau-ratio-key-metrics-for-assessing-user-engagement) vs. [Heap](https://www.heap.io/), it’s clear that both tools serve the purpose of improving [user experience](https://www.adasight.com/partner/amplitude) and providing valuable insights through analytics. However, different features and functionalities set both tools apart.

## Amplitude Analytics

Amplitude analytics tool entered the product analytics market in 2012 and has been a favorite of [big corporations](https://amplitude.com/customers), including Ford, PayPal, and Atlassian. Amplitude’s features, such as user journey tracking, behavioral cohort analysis, and A/B testing, allow in-depth analysis and experimentation. It also offers maximum customization flexibility, where you can create custom dashboards or modify the existing ones. Apart from robust features, Amplitude also makes data governance a fundamental part of its ecosystem. It uses Taxonomy to organize data in a structured format, ensuring data centralization and security.

## Heap analytics vs. Amplitude

Heap is another analytics tool that offers automated event tracking and retroactive analysis. This allows for the automatic capture of user interactions with historical data analysis. Heap also offers cohort analysis but requires manual setup, unlike Amplitude. Huel, Brooklinen, and Fivetran are among Heap’s prominent [customers](https://heap.io/customer-stories).

### Features

Amplitude and Heap both are designed to extract deep insights from user data. However, their distinct features make them suitable for varied requirements. Below are the unique attributes of both analytics tools:

#### Amplitude Analytics

![Amplitude cohort analysis](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/6539206f401d118f32c193e5/66b71af98e1ced8b6bf17ed8_AD_4nXdJXMj_QZKs5LECWRGKMA1u4eBXTgNJfYipnDWo2ogrWb2cmoDgUaWkKluZb4end1kW4OTJOgRwjZmDEQOa1z5BZ1aHBNfq0AC5LqGU9MdEPCiJxFO-9OUj-AD6zNbnz5GDcPKwKJzUQO5gff4-cK-CJPjO.png)

*Source*

- **Behavioral Analytics: **Amplitude offers insights into customer behavior, such as personal preferences and the impact of marketing efforts, through user flow diagrams and retention analysis. This feature is often highlighted positively in many Amplitude analytics reviews.
- **User Segmentation: **Amplitude allows customers to be divided into smaller groups according to their characteristics, such as demographics and behavior patterns. This allows the launch of targeted campaigns and personalized interaction with customers.
- **Event Segmentation: **Amplitude allows you to add ten product-specific events and five default events. Event segmentation helps identify the top events, their frequency, and the user journey. A product click, ad click, and payment submission are a few examples of user events.
- **Advanced Analytics: **Advanced Amplitude data analytics tools, including funnels, cohort analysis, and storytelling, give insights into which marketing efforts result in conversion and revenue. Amplitude dashboards offer advanced features, including bulk filters, data ranges, email reports, behavioral cohorts, comments, and generating a public link, allowing you to customize your view.
- **Real-time Analytics: **Amplitude updates its analytics as soon as your data is updated. Real-time analytics keep you updated with your performance based on up-to-the-minute user interactions.
- **A/B Testing: **Amplitude’s A/B testing feature allows you to run marketing experiments using targeted tactics. You can customize the experiments by developing your hypothesis, choosing a relevant metric, and obtaining user authorization.
- **Product Paths and Conversion Funnels: **Amplitude gives insights into user navigation, such as the time required to make a purchase, and highlights where users tend to leave. This allows for fine-tuning your customer journeys through data-driven decisions.

#### Heap analytics

![Heap analytics features](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/6539206f401d118f32c193e5/66b71af823a0144bc9c19b9a_AD_4nXdUkT6mKbgzfgnsPmi2Xgw_vBMQt33gy5W9MG7JI5Lxw2lgqLhkVCgZ3q9Z5F9X5SAVUkRANWrmWLjOBFFT303fNJWYrf7Q4RZMi0dJ9zTm82eBqO6Q-qI_4V28leIVQrEAbkv9I3iRvFMavJLfvnxp7JWd.png)

*Source*

- **Real-time Analytics: **Heap tracks data in real-time and reflects changes in the user interface as soon as they occur.
- **Retroactive Analysis: **Heap automatically captures user interaction without manual setup. You can pick an event, and Heap will generate its analysis using historical data.
- **Event and User Segmentation: **Heap allows you to divide your customers based on their interaction, such as users who clicked on a certain product 10 times and their characteristics. This allows for a focused and personalized user experience.

## Choosing the best analytics tools for product managers

While both tools serve the same purpose, choosing the best product analytics software depends on various factors, including your business requirements and analytic needs. Amplitude product analytics is ideal for businesses looking for in-depth exploration and growth due to its structured approach and advanced analytics capabilities. On the other hand, Heap is suitable for rapid implementation and exploratory analysis.

Therefore, if you’re a [business with growing data and a large customer base](https://www.adasight.com/partner/amplitude), Amplitude product analytics is a scalable option. It effortlessly handles large data and highlights nuances in customer behavior. Conversely, if your team has limited skills and wants quick implementation, Heap will suit your needs. However, it comes with the expense of granular user segmentation and greater event tracking flexibility.

## FAQs:

### What is the difference between Heap and Amplitude?

Amplitude focuses on advanced real-time analytics, customization, and user experience, making it ideal for businesses that need detailed insights and scalable solutions.

Heap offers automatic event tracking and retroactive analysis for businesses seeking a quick start.

### Which of the two tools provides the best value against the price?

Amplitude offers a comprehensive feature set on paid plans, which might increase businesses' return on investment (ROI).

To obtain a pricing estimate for Heap’s paid plan, you need to sign up and install a code snippet. Therefore, accessing pricing information can be difficult and time-consuming.

### How Do Amplitude and Heap handle user data?

Amplitude provides you control over the definition of events and properties, making it a strong choice among analytics tools for business. Amplitude also adheres to industry standards and complies with relevant data protection regulations such as GDPR and CCPA.

Heap also prioritizes data security and collects vast user interaction data without requiring upfront event definitions. However, automatic data collection can lead to privacy challenges if not handled carefully.
